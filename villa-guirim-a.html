Chapter One · The Residence

A Timeless Countryside Escape.

Configuration4 BHK

Villa Guirim A is a considered answer to the Goan countryside — open-plan living surrounded by rice fields and swaying palms, where architecture recedes and the landscape takes the lead.

Conceived by architect Abhishek Verma, the villa draws on the susegad philosophy of slow, rooted living. Sun-drenched courtyards, polished kota floors, and handcrafted timber doors meet contemporary lines and discreet technology — a residence that is neither nostalgic nor loud.

Every room opens to the outside; every sightline is an unhurried one. The villa is designed to be lived in slowly, year after year, as the garden matures around it.

Chapter Three · Craft & Materials

An Honest Palette.

Built inGoa
Interior

Local Timber, Polished Kota, Bespoke Brass.

The interior palette was specified to age. Burmese teak joinery is left unsealed; kota stone floors are hand-polished on site; brass fittings are cast by a single family workshop in Karnataka. Nothing here is a catalog purchase.

Landscape

A Garden That Arrived Before Us.

Mature palms, curry leaf, moringa, and frangipani were catalogued on the plot before construction began — and the plan moves around them. Rainwater harvesting, greywater recycling, and a rooftop solar array keep the villa light on its surroundings.

Outdoor

A 28-Foot Pool, East-Facing.

The pool is oriented to catch the early sun and stay in shade by afternoon. Teak decking, a low-slung outdoor dining pavilion, and a small sunken fire pit extend the villa's life into the evening.
