Chapter One · The Residence

Elevated Coastal Living.

SettingSea-facing

Moonstone Villa is a study in quiet luxury — a refined sea-facing bungalow in Badem, where the breeze off the Arabian Sea becomes a resident of the house itself.

Designed by architect Meetu Akali, Moonstone draws its discipline from the coastline it faces. Clean white volumes, low-slung rooflines, and restrained fenestration all serve a single purpose: let the view, the light, and the sound of the sea do the work.

Inside, the palette is considered and spare — polished local stone, Burmese teak, and the soft whites one finds in old Goan churches. The villa is not trying to be anything other than an unhurried weekend home for a family that knows exactly what it wants from the coast.

Chapter Three · Craft & Materials

A Palette Drawn From the Coast.

Built inGoa
Architecture

Low, Long, and Facing the Sea.

The villa's horizontal lines and deep overhangs echo the coastal vernacular. Rooms are organised along a single long axis so every primary space looks toward the water. What you see from bed is what you see from the pool — the Arabian Sea, holding court.

Interior

Four Shades of White, One Warm Timber.

The interior is an exercise in restraint — a carefully edited palette where nothing competes with the view. Moonstone's owners asked for a home they could arrive at and simply exhale in; every material choice serves that brief.

Outdoor

A Pool That Reads as the Sea's Continuation.

The lap pool is aligned exactly on the horizon line of the sea beyond. Water meets water. In the morning, the owners take their coffee at the pool edge — and the view doesn't know where the villa ends and the ocean begins.
