Chapter One · The Residence

A Warmer Take on the Countryside.

Configuration3 BHK

Where Villa Guirim A is quiet and contemplative, Villa Guirim B is generous and welcoming — a villa for long lunches, gatherings that stretch into the night, and mornings that begin slowly around a courtyard.

Warm terracotta tones, arched openings, and hand-laid cement tiles give it a distinctly Indo-Portuguese character. The plan is organised around a central open-air courtyard, with the living pavilion on one side and the bedroom wing on the other — a layout as old as Goa itself, refined with contemporary rigor.

Every detail, from the scalloped arches to the hand-cast brass door pulls, has been drawn and redrawn until it belonged to this house alone.

Chapter Three · Craft & Materials

Indo-Portuguese, Reimagined.

Built inGoa
Floors

Hand-Laid Cement Tiles, Pattern-Matched.

Every tile in Villa Guirim B was cast and hand-laid by a Goan workshop that has made them the same way since 1932. The living pavilion's pattern is unique to this villa — drawn by the architect and carried in by hand.

Architecture

Arches That Belong to This Plot.

The scalloped arches that frame the courtyard were proportioned to the old Indo-Portuguese homes still standing within a kilometre of site. Nothing was copied — but the DNA is unmistakable.

Outdoor

A 24-Foot Pool, Flanked by Colonnades.

The pool is framed on one long side by a shaded colonnade — the kind of edge condition that invites slow afternoons with a book. Evenings shift to dinners under hand-wrought wrought-iron lanterns.
